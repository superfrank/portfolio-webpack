I'm Feeling Lucky

I'm Frank Hulley-Jones

I'm the design lead in visual journalism at the Guardian in London. Whether it be breaking news, historic events or scientific discoveries, I love bringing the web to life through code, animation and illustration. My aim is to create engaging digital experiences that tell stories and excite audiences from across the world.

Here are three things you should know about me:

I design with the audience in mind

It could be offering some respite from a global crisis, or building a supporter site to encourage users to contribute — usability and accessibility is at the centre of it all. I approach each project in the same way: thorough research, data-informed decisions, iteration and asking for feedback from anyone and everyone.

I seek collaboration

Design is about storytelling and the best way to tell a good story is through teamwork — whether it is working alongside UX researchers, animators, writers and fellow coders to make the most out of their expertise, or mentoring designers and engineers to help them reach their potential.

I relish the opportunity to build new experiences that surprise, thrill and evoke (and sometimes even win awards ).

I have a distinctive voice

With each project, I try to bring elements of playfulness into my work — It could be through a charming illustration, clever animation or hidden idea.

People have such little time and such short attention spans, so if I create something that rewards them for pausing and exploring, they will become more engaged, intrigued and willing to dedicate the little time they have.
