The internet but not as we know itLive article

At the end of 2018 more than half of the world's population went online. Tim Berners-Lee raised a glass, whilst I spent 6 weeks trying to figure out how Chinese Microblogging worked.

The brief for the project was: “What’s life look like online in India, Cuba, China and Russia? Make it as sexy and maverick as possible.”

Taking this as a green light to design something slightly crazy, we set to work; starting with India.

India

With over 200 million users of WhatsAppIndia was a great place to start, as using mobile chat messages as a device let readers know this isn’t a regular news article and starts them scrolling

Extra touchThe time of the messages were at your computer's time

China

I wanted this section to feel like China was blocking western media but that you almost forget because of the alternatives offered. There’s a strong focus on animation as a reference to Weibo (a popular microblog)

Cuba

This was where the idea for pixelated design came from as we got given screenshots of “The weekly packet” which was running on an old version of Windows. The design heavily leans on how Windows 3.11 looked and functioned

Russia

A recreation of VK the Facebook clone. Has little touches like being able to like each post, or add The Kremlin as a possible friend
